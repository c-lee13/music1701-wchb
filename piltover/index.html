What Could Have Been

Piltover

City of Progress

 

In Arcane, Piltover glitters with innovation, wealth, and technological marvels, standing as a monument to capitalism and scientific ambition. Rich and resourceful, the city thrives on invention and industry, but success within its walls is far from meritocratic. Instead, it hinges on social standing -- those born into privilege, wealth, and influence dominate every sphere, while the rest are left to navigate a system rigged against them.

Piltover is governed by a council composed of elite families, many of whom have held power for generations. As Kublawi writes in "Arcane: The Perfect Pairing of Music and Animation", such systems “sustain themselves through inherited capital and institutional control, often masquerading as progress while maintaining exclusion” (Kublawi 2022). Manipulation, backdoor deals, and political favoritism are the true engines of governance in Piltover, and new power players must either play along or be silenced.

Meanwhile, the city’s citizens remain largely ignorant of the suffering in Zaun, the undercity below, where Piltover’s prosperity is built on the backs of the oppressed. Prejudice against Zaunites is deeply ingrained -- not always through overt hatred, but through internalized narratives of superiority and progress. Discrimination is the unspoken norm, and Piltover’s gleaming façade conceals a rot of apathy, injustice, and classist delusion. It is a dystopia draped in gold, blind to the rebellion it’s breeding just beneath its feet.
