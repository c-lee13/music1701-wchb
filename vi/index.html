What Could Have Been

Vi

The Lover and The Fighter

 

Vi’s story in Arcane is one of grief, identity, and aching love shaped by the relentless hardships of life in Zaun. She lost her parents at a young age and was raised by Vander, the moral anchor of the undercity. Growing up in his image, Vi became a fierce protector and natural leader—someone the other children looked up to despite her youth. Nowhere was her sense of responsibility stronger than in her bond with her younger sister, Powder, whom she loved deeply and tried desperately to shield from the violence around them.

But after a rebellion gone wrong and the devastating death of Vander, Vi's world falls apart. She lashes out at Powder in a moment of anger, blaming her for everything, just before being captured and imprisoned for years. When she finally returns, she finds her sister has become Jinx—unrecognizable, unstable, and consumed by a version of the world Vi no longer understands.

Through it all, Vi remains a lover at heart—driven not by revenge, but by longing, guilt, and an enduring hope that love might still save what’s left. Her story is about the pain of change, the weight of responsibility, and the tragic cost of love when it's left too long unspoken.
