What Could Have Been

Zaun

The Undercity

 

Burdened by pollution and poverty, Zaun is a brutal society where survival of the fittest is not just a norm, but a necessity.

For decades, Piltover's unchecked industrial development and toxic runoff have been filtered directly to Zaun, poisoning its air and swamping it with disease and sickness. Furthermore, the lack of political representation or a unified governing body leaves Zaun to anarchy, creating a relentless struggle for power that feeds into violence and discord. This abuse thus breeds a deep resentment among Zaunites toward Piltover, whose prosperity is built on their suffering, which creates numerous uprisings attempting reclaim autonomy from Piltover’s oppression.

At the head of these uprisings is Silco -- the leader of many of Zaun's prominent gangs. He represents the brains of the Undercity, advocating for radical independence through manipulation and ruthless tactics that often cause bloodshed. Most prominently, Silco sponsors Singed (a mad scientist) to create a drug that turns humans into physically-enhanced monsters, with the purpose of waging war upon Piltover. 

On the other hand, Vander -- Vi and Jinx's father-figure -- leads a separate crusade: not to battle for independence, but to preserve what little peace and stability remain in Zaun. This idealistic notion is short-lived, as Vander soon, too, becomes a victim of violence.

Relation to Piltover:

Piltover's systemic abuse -- the cause of great pollution and poverty -- has long since fractured any semblance of order within Zaun. With bloodshed being the only path to overthrowing Piltover, violence becomes not only a means of survival but also the sole way for Zaun to achieve autonomy.
